## Section 4 — Format & tone rules (the house style)

**Rule 1 — Opening line is an INR number or a verb.**
- Do: `₹2.3L recovered in 6 hours on 14-day-overdue invoices.`
- Don't: `Excited to share that we helped a client recover some money...`

**Rule 2 — No emoji in line 1, ever. Emoji in line 2+ only if it replaces a word.**
- Do: `DSO 47 → 22 days.`
- Don't: `🚀 Big news! DSO down massively 💰`

**Rule 3 — Real screenshots beat designed graphics 3–5×.**
- Do: Actual GSC screenshot with our cursor still visible.
- Don't: Canva-designed "results card" with stock-style icons.
- Never: AI-generated images. The audience smells them and discounts the entire post.

**Rule 4 — Hashtags: 0 in original tweets. 1 max in live-event threads only.**
- Do: live Conversations thread uses `#MetaConversations2026` once on anchor.
- Don't: `#WhatsAppBusiness #IndianSaaS #Bangalore #AI #Startup` (instant credibility tax).

**Rule 5 — Threads only when the post genuinely needs 3+ tweets to make sense.**
- Allowed: live-event coverage, full client post-mortem with chart sequence.
- Not allowed: "10 lessons from..." or anything that could fit in one tweet but got padded.

**Rule 6 — Tag max 2 accounts per tweet. Tag only when they will reasonably engage.**
- Do: tag the OP you're replying to. That's it.
- Don't: `cc @founder1 @founder2 @founder3 @brand` (cargo-cult Bengaluru-Twitter behaviour).

**Rule 7 — INR figures always with ₹ symbol and Indian numbering.**
- Do: `₹2.3L`, `₹38k`, `₹1.4 Cr`
- Don't: `Rs 2.3 lakhs`, `230,000 INR`, `$2.8K`

**Rule 8 — When uncertain about a claim, label it.**
- Do: `Anonymised post-mortem — Bengaluru salon chain, directional figures.`
- Don't: ever attribute a number to a named client without written permission.

2. **No public criticism of named competitors** (AiSensy, WATI, Interakt, Gupshup, DoubleTick, etc.). Category-level commentary is fine. Named-shaming is not.
