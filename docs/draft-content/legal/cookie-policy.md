# Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how ZentroTECH ("we," "us," "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on **zentrotech.com** (the "Site"). It works alongside our [Privacy Policy](./privacy-policy.md).

We aim to use as few cookies as possible — only what's needed to make the Site work well and, with your consent, to learn how to improve it.

## 1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that a website places on your device (computer, phone, tablet) when you visit. They let the site remember things about your visit — like your preferences, whether you're logged in, or which pages you've seen — so the next page load or next visit can be smoother.

We also use a few related technologies that work like cookies:

- **Local storage / session storage** — stored in your browser, similar to cookies
- **Pixels and beacons** — tiny image requests used to record events
- **Server-side analytics events** — recorded by our hosting provider without setting a cookie on your device

For convenience, we refer to all of these as "cookies" in this policy.

## 2. Types of Cookies We Use

We group cookies into three categories. You can accept or reject categories through our cookie banner.

### 2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies (always on)

These cookies are essential for the Site to function. They cannot be switched off. They typically only set themselves in response to actions you take — such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms.

| Cookie / item | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| `cookie_consent` | Stores your cookie banner choice so we don't ask again on every page | 12 months |
| Session cookie (Vercel edge) | Keeps your session connected to the right server during a visit | Session |
| CSRF / security tokens | Prevents cross-site request forgery on contact forms | Session |

No consent is required for strictly necessary cookies. Without them, parts of the Site will not work.

### 2.2 Performance Cookies (Vercel Analytics)

These cookies help us understand how visitors use the Site so we can improve it — which pages are popular, where people get stuck, how fast pages load.

We use **Vercel Analytics**, a privacy-friendly analytics tool from our hosting provider. Vercel Analytics:

- Does **not** use third-party cookies
- Does **not** track you across other sites
- Does **not** sell or share your data with advertisers
- Collects anonymised, aggregated metrics (page views, country-level location, device type, referrer)

| Item | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Vercel Analytics event ping | Aggregated, anonymised page-view and Web Vitals metrics | Not stored on your device |

### 2.3 Functional Cookies

If you accept functional cookies, we may remember preferences such as:

- Theme (dark / light mode, if offered)
- Reduced-motion preference
- Preferred language (when multi-language is enabled)

| Cookie | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| `theme` | Remembers your theme choice | 12 months |
| `motion_pref` | Remembers your motion-reduction choice | 12 months |
| `lang` | Remembers your language choice (when applicable) | 12 months |

These cookies do not track you across sites and are not used for advertising.

## 3. Third-Party Cookies

We try to keep third-party cookies to a minimum. Where we use them, they are set by partners we have vetted:

- **Vercel Analytics** (hosting provider) — described above; no device cookies set in the standard configuration.
- **Google Analytics** — only loaded if you opt in via the cookie banner. Sets cookies prefixed with `_ga`, `_gid`, `_gat` to measure traffic. We enable IP anonymisation and disable advertising features.
- **Calendar booking embed** (e.g., Cal.com or Calendly, used on the Contact page) — when you load that section, the embed may set cookies under its own domain to manage your booking session. Their cookie policies apply to those cookies.
- **YouTube / Vimeo embeds** (if used in case studies) — set cookies to track video playback. We use privacy-enhanced embed modes where available.

We do not use cookies from advertising networks, social-media trackers, or data brokers.

## 4. How to Control Cookies

You have several ways to control cookies on our Site.

**Our cookie banner.** When you first visit the Site, you'll see a banner letting you Accept All, Reject Non-Essential, or Customise by category. You can change your choice at any time via the **"Cookie Preferences"** link in the Site footer.

**Your browser.** Most browsers let you view, delete, or block cookies through their settings. Helpful links:

- [Chrome](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647)
- [Firefox](https://support.mozilla.org/kb/cookies-information-websites-store-on-your-computer)
- [Safari](https://support.apple.com/guide/safari/manage-cookies-sfri11471/mac)
- [Edge](https://support.microsoft.com/microsoft-edge/delete-cookies-in-microsoft-edge-63947406-40ac-c3b8-57b9-2a946a29ae09)

**Do Not Track / Global Privacy Control.** Where your browser sends a Do Not Track header or a Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal, we treat it as a request to disable non-essential cookies for that browser.

**Opting out of Google Analytics specifically.** You can install the [Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout) to prevent your data being used by Google Analytics.

Blocking strictly necessary cookies may break parts of the Site. Blocking performance and functional cookies will not break the Site, but some preferences may not be remembered between visits.
